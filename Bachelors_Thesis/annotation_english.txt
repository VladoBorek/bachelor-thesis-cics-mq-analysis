IBM mainframes generate large volumes of performance data
through the System Management Facility (SMF) framework, yet
extracting meaningful insights from this data remains
challenging due to its complexity and limited tool
accessibility. This thesis focuses on analyzing SMF records
from the Customer Information Control Systems (CICS) and IBM
Message Queueing (MQ) subsystems and proposes reusable
guidelines for their interpretation, even without commercial
analysis tools.

The work combines theoretical background with practical data
processing, including the development of a parser for CICS SMF
110 records and a structured approach to performance analysis.
Concepts such as the transaction lifecycle and message flow are
used to contextualize system behavior. Due to limitations in
the available dataset, the MQ-related analysis is based
primarily on theory.

The thesis contributes a set of actionable approaches for SMF
data analysis and aims to support both newcomers and those
experienced with mainframes in understanding and analysing CICS
and MQ system performance.
